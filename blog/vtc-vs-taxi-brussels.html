When the Taxi Still Makes Sense

Honestly, taxis still have their use cases:

  • Immediate street hail — you didn't plan ahead and one happens to drive by
  • Very short city centre rides — under 2 km, the meter might be cheaper
  • Night owls without a smartphone — rare, but they still exist

For everything else — airport, business, events, long distance, planned rides — the VTC is clearly superior.

FAQ — VTC vs Taxi Brussels

What is the difference between a VTC and a taxi in Brussels?

A taxi is hailed on the street or via a dispatch centre, with a meter. A VTC is booked in advance, with a fixed price known before the ride. The VTC also offers superior comfort: premium vehicle, professional chauffeur, onboard WiFi.

Is a VTC more expensive than a taxi in Brussels?

Not necessarily. A taxi has a variable price depending on traffic (meter). A VTC has a fixed price agreed in advance. During rush hour or for long journeys, the VTC is often cheaper. And the comfort is superior.
