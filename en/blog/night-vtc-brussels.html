Night VTC Brussels: why a private chauffeur is safer than a street taxi

Brussels never truly sleeps. Afterwork drinks in the Quartier Louise, late dinner in the Marolles, a concert at the Botanique or a night out at Place du Jeu de Balle… When night falls, the same question comes up: how do you get home safely? **Street taxi or professional VTC?** The answer might surprise you.

Here's why more and more Brussels locals — and visitors — are choosing **private VTC chauffeurs** for their late-night rides.

The problem with street taxis at night

Prices skyrocket after 10 PM

In Brussels, traditional taxis apply a **night surcharge** starting at 10 PM (and on weekends and public holidays). The meter runs faster, and a 15-minute daytime ride can easily cost **30 to 50 % more** at night. Not to mention the wait — finding a free taxi after a night out is often next to impossible.

No guarantee on comfort or quality

Brussels taxis are regulated, but quality varies enormously from one vehicle to the next. Common complaints: poorly maintained vehicle, unfriendly driver, a route that's "coincidentally" longer. At night, tired or after a few drinks, you're less vigilant — and some drivers know it.

The safety concern

Standing alone at 2 AM on a Brussels sidewalk, waiting for an anonymous taxi… It's not a pleasant experience. Women in particular often mention feeling uneasy. You don't know the driver. You don't know who they are, or whether the vehicle is actually affiliated with a licensed company.
