Brussels Charleroi Airport Taxi: The Complete VTC Transfer Guide

Brussels South Charleroi Airport (CRL) sits about 60 km from central Brussels. It's the main hub for several low-cost airlines, and every year thousands of Belgian and international passengers pass through its gates. But one question keeps coming back: **how do you get from Charleroi to Brussels reliably and comfortably?**

Train, bus, rideshare… there's no shortage of options, but they all come with trade-offs. Here's why more and more travellers are choosing a **professional VTC** for this journey — and how to book the right one.

Why Charleroi Airport is a special case

A remote airport with poor nighttime connections

Unlike Brussels Airport (Zaventem), Charleroi has no direct train link. You need to take the TEC bus to Charleroi station, then catch a train to Brussels — that's **1h30 to 2 hours of travel** with connections.

Low-cost flights tend to land late in the evening or depart early in the morning. At those hours, public transport options are scarce or non-existent. The result: you're left searching for a taxi on the spot, with no guarantee of price or quality.

The unofficial taxi trap

Outside Charleroi Airport, informal drivers sometimes offer rides at inflated prices. Without a pre-booked ride, you have **no protection** on the fare or the standard of the vehicle.

When a VTC is the smartest choice

Late arrival or early departure

Ryanair and Wizz Air flights often land after 10 PM. The last TEC bus departs around midnight. Miss it and you're stranded. A VTC waits for you, regardless of the hour.

Family travel

With children, pushchairs and suitcases, juggling bus connections and train platforms is a logistical nightmare. A private vehicle drops you right at your door.

Business travel

Got a meeting the next morning? No time to spend two hours on public transport. A VTC lets you work or rest during the journey.

Group of friends or colleagues

From 2–3 passengers onwards, splitting the VTC fare makes it **competitive** with the train + bus combo. And the comfort is in a different league.
